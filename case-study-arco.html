Case Study

ARCO

A gamified music education platform designed to help young violin students stay engaged with practice through rewards, progress tracking, and a playful interface.

Project Overview

ARCO combines a physical workbook with a digital learning experience. Students scan QR codes to unlock online activities, track progress, and earn rewards, making practice feel more interactive and motivating.

The Problem

Traditional practice methods can feel repetitive for young music students. Without clear progress tracking or engaging feedback, it can be difficult to maintain motivation over time.

Target Users

  • Beginner violin students ages 5–11
  • Parents supporting practice at home
  • Teachers guiding lesson progress

Research & Insights

  • Students respond well to visible rewards and milestones
  • Simple navigation is important for younger users
  • Progress indicators help practice feel more manageable
  • Digital interaction can strengthen engagement with workbook activities

Design Goals

  • Create a playful, approachable interface for children
  • Make progress and rewards easy to understand
  • Connect physical workbook activities to digital experiences
  • Keep interactions simple and intuitive

Wireframes

Wireframe Placeholder

Early wireframes focused on reducing clutter and making the main learning actions easy to find. The structure was designed to support quick access to lessons, rewards, and progress.

Final UI

Final UI Placeholder

The final interface uses clear visual hierarchy, playful styling, and simple navigation patterns to create a friendly experience for young users. Color, spacing, and icon use were chosen to support readability and engagement.

Outcome

ARCO was designed to make practice more engaging by giving students a sense of progress and accomplishment. The final concept supports motivation through rewards, visual feedback, and a more interactive learning flow.

Reflection

If I continued developing this project, I would expand user testing and refine the reward system based on feedback from students, teachers, and parents. I would also continue improving accessibility and interaction clarity.
